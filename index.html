Muster — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 29, 2026  ·  Effective date: June 29, 2026

Plain-language summary: Muster keeps your information on your own device. We do not collect it, we do not transmit it, we do not store it on any server, and we have no account system. What you track in Muster stays with you, and you decide if and when to share it.

Who this applies to

This policy covers the Muster mobile application ("Muster," "the app," "we," "our"). Muster is a personal readiness-tracking tool for individual service members.

The short version

Muster is built on a simple principle: your data is yours, and it stays on your device. Muster has no backend server, no user accounts, and no analytics that identify you. We cannot see your data because it never leaves your phone unless you choose to share it.

What information Muster stores

Muster stores the following locally on your device only:

  • Your profile information that you enter (such as name, rank, and unit designators like company, platoon, or squad).
  • The readiness items you track and the dates you log.

This information is saved in the app's local storage on your device. It is not transmitted to us or to any third party. We do not have access to it.

What information Muster does NOT collect

  • We do not collect your data on any server.
  • We do not require or create a user account.
  • We do not use third-party analytics or advertising trackers.
  • We do not collect your location.
  • We do not collect your device identifiers for tracking.
  • We do not sell or share your information with anyone, because we never receive it in the first place.

Sharing — only when you choose

Muster includes a feature that lets you generate a QR code summarizing your readiness, which you can show to a leader to scan in person. When you do this:

  • The QR code is generated on your device.
  • It is displayed on your screen for someone to scan in person.
  • Muster does not transmit this information over the internet. The sharing is a deliberate, in-person action you take, screen to screen.

What the QR code contains. The QR code summarizes the readiness information you already track in Muster:

  • Your name and rank.
  • Your unit designators (such as company, platoon, and squad), if you have entered them.
  • The date the code was generated.
  • For each readiness item you track: the item, its current status (for example, current, due soon, or overdue), and the relevant date (such as when you last completed it). Optional items you have not engaged are not included.

This is the same readiness picture you see in your own app — it does not include identifiers like a Social Security number or DoD ID number, contact information, or any clinical or medical details beyond the status of a readiness item.

A leader who scans readiness QR codes stores that information locally on their own device to view their formation's readiness. Muster automatically deletes this shared information from the leader's device after 96 hours. It is held only on that device, is not sent to us or to any server, and is removed automatically once the 96-hour period passes.

The camera

Muster uses your device camera only to scan QR codes shown by other users, and only when you open the scanning feature. Camera images are processed on the device to read the QR code and are not stored, saved, or transmitted.

Data retention and deletion

Because your data lives only on your device, you are in full control of it. You can delete individual entries within the app at any time. Deleting the app from your device removes all data Muster has stored. We retain nothing, because we never had it.

Information you share to a leader's device via QR is automatically deleted from that device after 96 hours (see "Sharing — only when you choose").

Children's privacy

Muster is intended for adult service members and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. (Muster does not collect information from anyone on a server in any case.)

Security

Muster's primary security protection is architectural: your information stays on your device and is not transmitted to or stored on any server, so there is no central database to breach. Information stored on your device is protected by your device's own operating-system security and encryption. You should protect your device with a passcode or biometric lock.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above and post the revised policy. Material changes will be reflected in an app update.
